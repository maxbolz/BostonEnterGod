So What?
The hypothesis of this experiment was that the number of churches in Boston, and the change thereof, would correspond with the difference in religious demographic data between the two time periods.
In A Nationwide Sense
The United States has reported a 12% decrease in Christianity between the two time periods, according to census information. Even though Massachusetts is decently Christian, the number of churches has decreased by 3 TIMES this amount. According to 1902 census information, there were 196,670 churches in the United States in 1906. Now, there are 380,000 churches in the United States. Even though this is a stark increase, comparing the number of churches to the population says otherwise. In 1906, there were 433.8 churches per person. Now, there is 885.2 churches per person in the U.S.; a 50% INCREASE from 1906!
In Conclusion
The nationwide average is not reflective of Boston's! Therefore, Boston is a unique case when it comes to religion. From this, we are able to conclude that Boston truly IS growing as a cultural center, as against all odds and statistics of the United States, it has proved that cultural diversity is still blooming. This makes sense, and can also be backed up by the simple fact that Massachusetts was primarily settled by Puritans; as time went on, only another mass migration could prevent a decrease in Christianity.
